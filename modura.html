Modura

This was a project I worked on while undertaking Design Lab’s UX Academy Foundations course in 2020, relating to visual and UI design. I was asked to design all UI elements from scratch using Figma (logo, icon set, color palette, typography, style tile), and the final product involved producing three mobile application screens.

Design brief

Modura is a new eCommerce clothing brand designed for trendy men and women between 18 and 25 years old. These individuals are early in their careers and are looking for an affordable solution for business casual clothing to wear to their jobs in urban areas. They are fashion-forward, and conscious of trends. They want to dress for the jobs they want without appearing overdressed.

My process

Logo design

I started by brainstorming and sketching a variety of logos for Modura by considering wordmark versions, monogram versions and logomark versions that relate to associated words such as “trendy”, “young”, “driven” and “transition”. I then chose 2 logos that I wanted to pursue and digitize and created responsive configurations of each, considering typography, spacing and aesthetics.

Icon design

I created a sample set of 4 icons in a style that would complement the style of the logo to enhance brand recognition (appearance, weight and dimensions), while also ensuring that each logo is recognizable in a small icon size. Text labels were added to add clarity about the icon’s meaning and purpose.

Color palette

I created two color palettes based on my knowledge of color theory and consideration of the emotions that certain colors can evoke. I narrowed down to (i) a muted and pastel scheme including a relatively dark red, which is associated with confidence, while also using yellow as an accent/action color to add interest and playfulness so it does not feel overly formal and (ii) a refreshing light green color palette to represent the fresh, younger target audience as well as some hints of dark green to make it slightly more formal. I ended up choosing the first option as I felt the color scheme would be a more pleasant and calming experience and the pastel color scheme works well for a professional but not overly formal brand.

Typography

The goal was to choose a front to suit the tone of Modura. I came up with a serif header and sans-serif body option, as well as a single sans-serif font option for easy readability. Initially I decided to proceed with the former, however after experimenting with the fonts when designing the app itself, I thought having two fonts added unnecessary complexity to the text hierarchy, so I decided to proceed with the second sans-serif option instead for the app.

Style tile

I compiled all the UI assets into a style tile to check that all the pieces work well together before diving into the design of the app interface itself.

Final app design

I designed a (i) sign in/sign up screen, (ii) home screen showing latest offers and (iii) screen showing details of an individual item of clothing.

The iterations and changes I made from the first version to the last version consisted of the following:

  • Ensuring consistency in typography and color across all call-to-action buttons, and de-emphasizing non call-to-action buttons (including category buttons and footer arrows)
  • Adjusting footer and icon size for a more minimalistic and professional look
  • Responsive design changes - rearranging the individual clothing item screen so that the elements are displayed vertically (for mobile) instead of horizontally (which would be more suitable for a desktop version)
  • Allowing the option to see more reviews
  • Addition of the titles of the related clothing items
  • Using thinner strokes and dividers for a more professional look
